Breast cancer remains the leading cause of cancer-related death among women worldwide. While early-stage interventions targeting the primary tumor have improved, strategies to prevent or treat aggressive breast cancer and metastasis remain less effective. Tumor dissemination is a major contributor to mortality in breast cancer patients.

Dr. Reynoird‚Äôs team observed that BCAR3 methylation by the lysine methyltransferase SMYD2 enhances cancer cell migration and invasiveness. Their work demonstrated that SMYD2 contributes to breast cancer metastasis and that BCAR3 methylation facilitates its interaction with the actin nucleator FMNL3.

In this project, we investigated the role of BCAR1 in the SMYD2‚ÄìBCAR3 pathway by assessing whether BCAR1/BCAR3 perturbation impacts cytoskeletal remodeling. We found that BCAR1 and BCAR3 are expressed and interact in aggressive breast cancer cell lines. Using specific mutants, we demonstrated that BCAR1 interacts with FMNL3 through BCAR3, highlighting the importance of this complex in cytoskeletal dynamics.

üß¨ Graphical Abstract

BCAR1 interacts with BCAR3 (1), which undergoes SMYD2 methylation at lysine 334 (2). This modification facilitates recruitment of formin-like proteins (FMNLs). Upon binding to methylated BCAR3, FMNLs become activated, enhancing actin polymerization (3). Increased actin assembly promotes plasma membrane extensions and lamellipodia formation, contributing to cellular migration.
